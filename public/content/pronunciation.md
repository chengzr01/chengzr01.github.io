# Pronunciation

*March 2026*

My name is **Zirui Cheng** (程子睿). Following the Chinese name order, it is written as Chéng Zǐruì in Pinyin. In Standard Mandarin, the pronunciation may be represented in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) as: **[ʈʂʰəŋ tsɿ ʐui]**. For English speakers, a rough approximation would be something like *Chung Dzrway*, though several sounds in Mandarin differ systematically from their closest English counterparts.

## The vowel i

A common source of confusion is the pronunciation of the letter i, which does not always correspond to the vowel [i] in Standard Mandarin. Instead, it represents three different sounds depending on the preceding consonant.

- **[i] (high front unrounded vowel).** This is the regular vowel familiar from many languages. Examples include *mi* [mi] and *li* [li].

- **[ɿ] (apical vowel after z, c, s).** After the alveolar sibilants z, c, s, the letter i represents an apical vowel produced with the tongue tip raised toward the alveolar ridge rather than with the tongue body forming the typical [i] configuration. Examples include *zi* [tsɿ], *ci* [tsʰɿ], and *si* [sɿ].

- **[ʅ] (apical vowel after zh, ch, sh, r).** After the retroflex initials zh, ch, sh, r, the vowel is another apical vowel articulated further back in the mouth. Examples include *zhi* [ʈʂʅ], *shi* [ʂʅ], and *ri* [ʐʅ].

The sounds [ɿ] and [ʅ] are often referred to as *apical vowels* because their articulation primarily involves the tongue tip rather than the tongue body. From a phonological perspective, these sounds occur in complementary distribution, which is why Pinyin represents them all with the single letter i.

## The consonants z, c, s and zh, ch, sh

Mandarin distinguishes two sets of sibilant consonants that differ primarily in place of articulation. The consonants **z, c, s** are alveolar sounds produced with the tip of the tongue near the alveolar ridge: *z* [ts] (unaspirated alveolar affricate), *c* [tsʰ] (aspirated alveolar affricate), and *s* [s] (alveolar fricative). In contrast, **zh, ch, sh** are retroflex sounds produced with the tongue tip curled upward and slightly retracted: *zh* [tʂ] (unaspirated retroflex affricate), *ch* [tʂʰ] (aspirated retroflex affricate), and *sh* [ʂ] (retroflex fricative).
